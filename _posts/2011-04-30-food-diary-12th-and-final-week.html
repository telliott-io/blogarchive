Tea's retained it's top spot in spectacular fashion in this final week of food photography.

I've decided to bring this little experiment in public guilt-tripping this week. I have three reasons for stopping now:

  1. I've probably got a good idea of my habits by now.
  2. Manually tagging every photo and doing this weekly blog is taking up time I could be doing new, more interesting things.
  3. Next week would be week 13, and I'm superstitious.

That's not to say it hasn't been worthwhile. I'm drinking more water, started taking my coffee black, finally convinced myself that I don't need bread to enjoy soup, and written a funky little bit of JavaScript for making animated histograms from Flickr sets. That last one I might be able to do something more with.

My tea habit's in full force this week, with a shocking 47 cups. That's about 7 a day! But I think this was down to a combination of visiting family for a chunk of the week, and being off work, where at least 3 of those daily cups would have been replaced by black coffee. To put it in electoral terms, the caffeine vote wasn't split as it had been in the past.

But what do I do now? Well, I suppose my aims will be pretty standard: drink more water, drink less alcohol and caffeine and avoid snacking between meals. Given that I lost nearly half a stone with a couple of cheese-free weeks, I should probably steer clear of that where possible as well. Perhaps have a few cheeseless days every week.

I hope this hasn't bored you all to tears with endless photos of hot drinks. Who knows, maybe it's inspired you to think a bit more about what passes your lips. Or even take on a photographic challenge of your own.

Happy eating, drinking and other mandatory life activities!
